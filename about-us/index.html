About Us

The “Bromley Community Choir” is a musical group designed to bring people together.
Socialise with a  purpose – meet a wide range of people in a safe and friendly atmosphere.
 In joining us, your confidence in singing will improved, your talent is recognised and your ability to interact in a friendly and safe environment expands.
Our base in the Bromley Central Library is designed to provide a central location suitable for all. We are accessible as there is a lift to the 5th Floor.
We accept singers of all ages but please note that under 16s must be accompanied by a parent or other responsible adult.
No special music skills are required as training is given, whilst those who have musical training or experience are welcome to join and display their ability and improve their skills.
Rehearsals are open to all residents of Bromley and are held at the Central Library on Thursday’s 7:pm to 9:pm.

History

It all began in October 2013 when Choir member Anthony Morris approached the manager of Bromley Town Hall to discuss ideas on how to get the community more socially active and interrelated.
 After a helpful discussion, Anthony was inspired to start a community choir which would aim to include people from all backgrounds.
He  further proposed to have the Lewisham Pathfinders Club and Drum Corps perform at the switch-on of the Christmas lights in Bromley High Street, after showing a video demonstrating their talents and capabilities.
The following year the BCC had their inaugural performance at this event just after six weeks after its formation in October 2014.  This was made possible through the dedication of our first choir director Margaret Clarke assisted by Camille Danzie.  Since then the choir continues to perform annually at this and other Christmas lighting ceremonies, care homes, festivals and special events.
“The Youth Community Marching Steel Band have been a regular feature at Bromley Town Centre events since 2012, their performances are electrifying! They have marched up and down the pedestrian area in Bromley at many of our events including the Poppy Appeal celebrations and the Christmas Lights Switch On event. They were an extremely popular feature with the community in general.
The Bromley Community Choir, also excellent, performed at the Bromley Christmas Lights Switch On event in 2015 attracting a wide audience.
Bromley Town Centre Manager”
